Join us as we delve into the bloodiest day in the Ancient World

On the 2nd of August 216 BC a Roman army the likes of which had never been seen, took to the field to face off against the existential threat of the Republic's greatest enemy, Hannibal Barca.

After his audacious march from Southern Spain through the Alps to surprise two Roman armies, one at Trebia and the other at Trasimene. Hannibal had shown he was a master of using the terrain to his advantage. This time the Romans caught him on an open field believing themselves to be infallible, they advanced into the jaws of a trap.

We will take a look at how the war itself came about, the battle itself and its aftermath as we explore the greatest loss of life in a single day in Western Europe until the Battle of the Somme.
